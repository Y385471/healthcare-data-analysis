
From Excel Sheets to Business Insights: Dental Clinic Performance Analysis
I recently worked on analyzing a dental clinic dataset and built a dashboard to evaluate financial performance, operational efficiency, and growth opportunities.
Key findings from the analysis:
The clinic generated $1.95M in total revenue and $1.47M in profit, resulting in an approximate 75% profit margin. This indicates strong cost control and efficient operations.
The collection rate reached 97%, reflecting a highly effective financial system with minimal revenue loss.
A total of 898 patients generated this revenue, with an average revenue per patient of approximately $2.1K. This shows that the business relies heavily on high-value treatments rather than high patient volume.
Implants and braces are the primary revenue drivers. Implants generated $838K in revenue and $606K in profit, making them the most valuable service. Braces contributed $647K in revenue but showed relatively higher costs compared to other treatments.
Other services such as fillings and extractions contributed minimally to overall profitability, indicating that they function more as entry-point services rather than profit drivers.
Key opportunity:
The clinic has strong potential for growth by increasing patient volume while maintaining the same level of operational efficiency.
Strategic recommendation:
Develop a structured patient funnel starting from OPD and X-ray, leading to diagnosis and conversion into high-value treatments such as implants and braces.
This project demonstrates how data analysis can directly support decision-making and business growth in healthcare.
